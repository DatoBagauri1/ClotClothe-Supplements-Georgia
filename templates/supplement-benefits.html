Benefits of Supplements: Creatine & Protein

Creatine: Boost Power and Brain Function

Creatine is one of the most researched supplements worldwide, known primarily for its ability to enhance muscle strength and power during high-intensity exercise. It increases phosphocreatine stores in your muscles, helping regenerate ATP, the key energy molecule.

Besides physical benefits, recent studies show creatine also supports brain health by improving memory and mental fatigue resistance.

  • Boosts muscle strength and endurance for explosive activities like sprinting and lifting.
  • Supports recovery by reducing muscle damage and inflammation.
  • Improves cognitive function in tasks requiring short-term memory and quick thinking.

Research Highlights

A comprehensive review by Kreider et al. (2017) shows creatine supplementation increases lean body mass and maximal strength in trained individuals.

Another study demonstrated cognitive benefits, especially under mental stress.

Protein: Essential for Muscle Growth & Recovery

Protein is a vital macronutrient that helps repair and build muscle tissue after workouts. It supplies amino acids needed to stimulate muscle protein synthesis, leading to increased muscle size and improved recovery times.

Consuming the right amount of protein daily, especially post-workout, is crucial for maximizing gains.

  • Whey protein is fast-digesting and rich in essential amino acids, ideal immediately after workouts.
  • Casein protein digests slowly, making it perfect before sleep to aid overnight muscle repair.
  • Plant-based proteins such as pea, rice, and hemp offer good alternatives for vegetarians and vegans.

Scientific Studies

The landmark study by Phillips et al. (2016) confirms 20-40g of protein post-exercise optimally stimulates muscle protein synthesis.

Another research highlights whey protein's unique amino acid profile, rich in leucine, the key driver of muscle growth.
